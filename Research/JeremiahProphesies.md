# Prophecies in the Book of Jeremiah

Here are the major prophecies found in the Book of Jeremiah:

1. Judgment against Judah and Jerusalem (1:11-19)
   - The vision of the almond branch and boiling pot
   - Jerusalem will be besieged by northern enemies

2. Babylon as HaShem's instrument of judgment (5:15-17; 20:4-6; 21:3-10)
   - Babylonian invasion and conquest predicted
   - 70-year exile for the people of Judah

3. The Temple's destruction (7:12-15; 26:6-9)
   - Jerusalem's temple will be destroyed like Shiloh
   - The city will become desolate

4. The fate of kings and leaders (21-22)
   - King Zedekiah will be captured by Babylonians (21:3-7)
   - King Jehoiakim will have a dishonorable burial (22:18-19)
   - King Jehoiachin (Coniah) will be childless on the throne (22:24-30)

5. False prophets will be punished (23:9-40; 28:15-17)
   - Specific prediction about the death of Hananiah within a year

6. The New Covenant prophecy (31:31-34)
   - HaShem will make a new covenant with Israel
   - Law written on hearts rather than stone

7. Purchase of land as sign of future restoration (32:6-15)
   - Jeremiah's purchase of field at Anathoth as prophetic sign

8. Return from exile after 70 years (25:11-12; 29:10-14)
   - Specific timeframe for exile and promise of restoration

9. Judgment against foreign nations (chapters 46-51)
   - Egypt (46)
   - Philistia (47)
   - Moab (48)
   - Ammon (49:1-6)
   - Edom (49:7-22)
   - Damascus (49:23-27)
   - Kedar and Hazor (49:28-33)
   - Elam (49:34-39)
   - Babylon (50-51)

10. The righteous Branch of David (23:5-6; 33:14-16)
    - Promise of a future righteous king from David's line

11. Eternal priesthood and kingship (33:17-26)
    - Davidic dynasty and Levitical priesthood will never end

12. Rebuilding and restoration of Jerusalem (30-33)
    - Known as the "Book of Consolation"
    - Promise of healing, rebuilding and prosperity
