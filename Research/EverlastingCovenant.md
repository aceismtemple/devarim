The phrase "everlasting covenant" (ברית עולם, brit olam) appears several times in the Tanakh. Here are the key verses:

**Genesis:**
- 9:16 - HaShem's covenant with Noah after the flood, symbolized by the rainbow
- 17:7 - HaShem's covenant with Abraham and his descendants
- 17:13 - The covenant of circumcision with Abraham
- 17:19 - HaShem's covenant specifically with Isaac

**Exodus:**
- 31:16 - The Sabbath as an everlasting covenant with Israel

**Leviticus:**
- 24:8 - The showbread as an everlasting covenant

**Numbers:**
- 18:19 - The gifts of the holy offerings to Aaron and his sons
- 25:13 - Phinehas and his descendants receiving an everlasting covenant of priesthood

**2 Samuel:**
- 23:5 - David speaks of HaShem making an everlasting covenant with him

**Isaiah:**
- 24:5 - Reference to the earth's inhabitants breaking the everlasting covenant
- 55:3 - HaShem's everlasting covenant, the faithful mercies of David
- 61:8 - HaShem will make an everlasting covenant with Israel

**Jeremiah:**
- 32:40 - HaShem promises to make an everlasting covenant with Israel
- 50:5 - A call to join in an everlasting covenant with HaShem

**Ezekiel:**
- 16:60 - HaShem will establish an everlasting covenant with Jerusalem
- 37:26 - HaShem will make a covenant of peace, an everlasting covenant

These covenants relate to different aspects of HaShem's relationship with humanity, Israel, and specific figures like Abraham and David.
